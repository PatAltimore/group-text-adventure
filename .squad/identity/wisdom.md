## 🧭 Core Principles of Great Interactive Fiction

### **1. Player agency must matter**
- Choices should create real consequences.  
- Avoid meaningless branches unless used intentionally.  
- Let players express different personalities.

### **2. Keep prose clear, vivid, and economical**
- Use strong verbs and sensory details.  
- Break text into short paragraphs.  
- Let players infer rather than over-explaining.

### **3. Build a world that reacts**
- Characters remember past actions.  
- Locations evolve over time.  
- Earlier discoveries unlock later possibilities.

### **4. Design choices around conflict**
- Good choices involve trade-offs.  
- Avoid trivial or obvious decisions.

### **5. Use pacing to control emotional rhythm**
- Alternate between exploration, decisions, crises, and cooldown moments.

---

## 🧩 Structural Guidance for Text-Based Adventures

### **6. Map your narrative like a tree—not a bush**
- Use branching paths that reconverge.  
- Track decisions with variables and flags.  
- Avoid exponential narrative sprawl.

### **7. Make puzzles serve the story**
- Tie puzzles to character motivations.  
- Ensure clues are fair and discoverable.  
- Offer multiple solutions when possible.

### **8. Give the player a clear goal**
- Objectives should always be understandable, even in mysterious settings.

---

## 🎭 Character & Dialogue Design

### **9. Let NPCs have agendas**
- Give them desires, fears, and secrets.  
- Let their behavior shift based on player actions.

### **10. Write adaptive dialogue**
- Branch responses based on past choices.  
- Let players choose tone.  
- Keep dialogue concise and purposeful.

---

## 🧱 Worldbuilding That Supports Interaction

### **11. Use environmental storytelling**
- Let objects and locations reveal history and mood.

### **12. Make items meaningful**
- Avoid inventory clutter.  
- Ensure items reinforce themes or character arcs.

---

## 🧪 Testing & Player Experience

### **13. Playtest for clarity**
Ask testers:
- Did you ever feel stuck?  
- Did choices feel meaningful?  
- Did the story surprise you?  
- Were your goals clear?

### **14. Fail forward, not dead-end**
- Let mistakes create consequences, not game overs.  
- Reserve hard fail states for dramatic effect.

---

## 🧠 Advanced Techniques

### **15. Use variables for personalization**
- Track trust, morality, knowledge, items, and past decisions.  
- Use these to alter descriptions, dialogue, or outcomes.

### **16. Foreshadow major branches**
- Hint at risks and consequences.  
- Use recurring symbols or motifs.

### **17. Build thematic cohesion**
- Choose a central theme.  
- Let choices reinforce it.  
- Use recurring imagery or emotional beats.

**Context:**
These principles and patterns are for creating interactive fiction. They are meant to guide design decisions and help teams create engaging, meaningful player experiences when creating worlds for the game. They are not strict rules, but rather heuristics to consider when building text-based adventures.
